StratShare

OVERIEW

StratShare is a ride-sharing application designed exclusively for students enrolled in the Global Business and Digital Arts program at the University of Waterloo. It addresses the challenges faced by those living in Waterloo but must commute to Stratford for their classes.


The idea for StratShare came about during our final assignment in GBDA 210: Intro to User Experience Design. We were tasked with the job of creating an application that would help individuals in our community.

Understanding the Problem
Many GBDA students, myself included, opt to reside near the University of Waterloo's main campus, drawn by the university life it offers. However, throughout my time in the GBDA program, I've encountered individuals expressing frustration about living away from home while essentially attending a "commuter school." They complain about the high costs associated with most ride-shares and the inconveniences linked to public transportation. Recognizing the importance of attending classes for academic success, we believe that every GBDA program participant should have access to an organized, affordable, and convenient means of reaching class, equipped with the necessary tools to do so.

User Testing
Defining User Problems
We conducted interviews with the target users of our application, specially GBDA students, to find out the problems they encountered when commuting from Waterloo to Stratford. The result shows that:
Reliability
Many GBDA students express challenges tied to depending on their carpool for transportation. If their carpool partner misses a day of school, it poses a hindrance to their own ability to attend.
Convenience
Challenges arise from rigid transportation schedules, such as the sole departure time of the bus at 7:45 am. Furthermore, commuting to the bus stop adds an additional layer of time commitment.
Affordability
Some find the cost of the bus program outweighs the convenience it offers. Additionally, students obligated to bring their own vehicles complain about parking fees and gas prices.
Accessibility
Due to the program's size, many are unfamiliar with others who share comparable schedules or have available car space. Students who drive find difficulties in finding potential passengers.

From interviewing GBDA students and analyzing alternate forms of commuting, we identified an opportunity to develop a platform that integrates both a community element and dependable ride-share scheduling. This solution aims to address the various challenges GBDA students currently face in their commuting experiences.


Which leads to the question...

 

"How might we create an affordable and community-centric ride-sharing platform to improve GBDA students' commuting experiences?"

Product Vision and Solution

Building on our insights, our product for the ride-share app tailored to GBDA students aligns with the following key business goals:

 

  • We want to assist students in discovering the most cost-effective commuting alternatives, while simultaneously offering students who drive a platform to earn money.
  • We want to enhance the overall commuting experience by providing users with seamless ride-sharing options that fit into their schedules and preferences.
  • We want to implement robust safety measures within the app, creating a secure environment for both drivers and passengers.

 

As a starting point, our team conducted thorough market research, examining alternative ride-share apps to gain insights and draw inspiration from features that enhance affordability, convenience, and safety.

Defining the MVP
Based on the sketching sessions, it was revealed that there were commonalties in the product vision. We identified the Minimum Viable Products (MVPs) following key user stories:
  • User Registration and Profiles. Allow GBDA students to create accounts with their details, and build user profiles to track commuting preferences and patterns.
  • Safety and Verification. Users are required to authenticate their UWaterloo identity through DUO security. Students registering as drivers must submit a valid driver's license.
  • Ride-sharing Scheduler. Develop a feature for users to plan and coordinate ridesharing.
  • Affordability Calculator. Integrate a tool to calculate and display the cost savings of ridesharing compared to individual commuting.
  • In-app messaging. Include a messaging system to facilitate communication between students.
  • Rewards and Compensation System. Create a system to reward and compensate students who drive and share rides, encouraging participation.
  • User Feedback Mechanism. Implement a feedback system for users to provide input on their commuting experiences and the app's functionality.
User flow & Wireframes

Key Takeaways
While creating our app for GBDA students, including ourselves, we initially assumed we understood all the commuter struggles. However, by interviewing a broader range of individuals, including different GBDA students and non-uWaterloo commuters, we gained valuable insights into additional pain points. This experience reinforced the importance of considering various viewpoints, resulting in a more inclusive and user-friendly design.

Effective design is powered by collaboration, where engaging with team members and potential users introduces varied viewpoints. This collaborative approach fosters innovation, resulting in a more comprehensive and well-rounded design.
