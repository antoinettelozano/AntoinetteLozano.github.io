Colour City

OVERVIEW


The Colour City Website Redesign project aimed to enhance the online presence and user experience for Colour City, a pottery painting business located in Markham, Ontario.


As an assignment for GBDA228: Web Design, the objective was to revamp the existing website, making it more visually appealing, user-friendly, and aligned with modern design standards. Through this redesign, I sought to create a digital platform that effectively showcases Colour City's offerings, encourages user engagement, and ensures a seamless experience for visitors interested in pottery painting activities.

The redesign of the Colour City website was essential to address outdated design elements and improve the overall user experience. The existing website lacked modern aesthetics and user-friendly features, hindering its ability to effectively showcase Color City's pottery painting services. The need for a redesign stemmed from the desire to align the digital presence of Color City with contemporary design standards, enhance visual appeal, and provide a seamless platform for users to explore and engage with the creative offerings of the pottery painting business. The goal was to create a refreshed and inviting online environment that would attract and retain visitors, ultimately driving business growth for this small business.

Heuristic Evaluation
In a comprehensive video critique, I conducted a heuristic analysis of the Color City website, meticulously examining its user interface and overall design. Through this analysis, I aimed to evaluate the website's adherence to established usability principles and identify areas for improvement.
Key Takeaways

 

Visual Design

  • Logo design is outdated
  • Lack of visual identity for pottery theme

Usability

  • Dispersed information
  • Small text size
  • Irrelevant and confusing content.

Content Quality

  • Text needs larger size for better readability.
  • Price list lacks clarity.
  • Inconsistent hours.
Defining the MVP
To identify the Minimum Viable Products for the website redesign, I generated a sitemap for the current website. Analyzing each page's content, I compiled a list of elements to retain and aspects that required modification.
Original Sitemap
  • Streamlined Navigation. Ensure an intuitive and simplified navigation structure, improving user experience and making it easy for visitors to find relevant information effortlessly.
  • Visual Identity Enhancement. Revise the visual elements, including the logo and overall aesthetics, to better reflect the creative and vibrant nature of Colour City, fostering a visually engaging and appealing online presence.
  • Content Clarity and Readability. Optimize content presentation by addressing text size and readability concerns, ensuring that information is clear and easy to comprehend for users.
  • Consistent and Accurate Information. Ensure accuracy in presenting business information, such as hours of operation, to avoid confusion and provide users with reliable details.
  • Visual Representation of Services. Incorporate visual elements, such as images of beautifully crafted pottery, to visually communicate the unique offerings of Colour City as a pottery painting business.
  • Conversion Path Optimization. Streamline the conversion path, particularly for actions like submitting email addresses or accessing social media accounts, to enhance user engagement and interaction with the brand.
User Flow
Leveraging insights from the established MVP criteria, I proceeded to craft an improved user flow and sitemap for the Colour City website redesign. The primary objective was to enhance user experience, streamline navigation, and create a visually appealing journey for visitors.

Key Takeaways
In revamping the Colour City website, prioritizing the needs of users was crucial. Identifying a key issue with the current site revealed its lack of appeal, especially to the predominantly younger audience interested in pottery painting. By adopting a user-centric approach, it became more feasible to develop a visually compelling identity that resonates with users and fosters engagement.
Considering the fact that I had complete creative freedom over this assignment, distinguishing between creative elements that might be deemed inconsequential and the essential components necessary for user navigation posed a challenge. In hindsight, a more effective approach would have involved establishing a clearer vision for the site's aesthetics through preliminary sketches or wireframes before diving directly into the website redesign on Figma. In the future, this would enable a more strategic and well-defined creative process.
