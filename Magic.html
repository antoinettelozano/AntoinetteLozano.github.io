Why was a redesign needed?
In my role as the marketing and design lead for the Student Council, I proposed the concept of revamping our logo. After seeking input from all members of the council and obtaining approval from our teacher supervisor, I assumed the responsibility of spearheading the logo redesign initiative. The original logo had remained unchanged since 2010, prompting the need for a fresh and modern representation. Thus, I undertook the task of redesigning the logo for Father Michael McGivney Catholic Academy, aiming to bring it up to date and align it with the evolving identity of our student community.

Original Logo Analysis
In conducting a thorough critique of the old logo design, I employed a discerning eye to identify its strengths and weaknesses. The evaluation process involved a comprehensive analysis of various design elements, considering both aesthetic and functional aspects.
  • Text and Typography. Text is old and outdated. The two different text types do not compliment each other
  • Outdated Aesthetics. The original logo design exhibits a dated visual style, with a lack of alignment with contemporary design trends. As design trends evolve, the original logo no longer aligns with modern branding.
  • Complexity and Clutter. The logo has complexity and visual clutter, hindering quick and clear recognition. Simplifying the design can enhance its impact.
  • Limited Scalability. The original design does not scale well across various sizes and platforms, leading to issues with visual coherence in different contexts, such as on digital platforms or merchandise.
Defining the MVP
  • Modernized Wizard Icon. Keep the wizard mascot as the central figure, but update its design with a contemporary touch.
  • Color Scheme Refinement. Maintain the existing colour palette, refining it to align with modern design trends.
  • Simplified Typography. Opt for clean and modern fonts, ensuring readability and a current aesthetic.
  • Responsive Design. Ensure the logo scales well across various platforms without losing clarity.
  • Timeless Quality. Aim for a modern design that retains a timeless quality, avoiding overly trendy elements.
  • Feedback Iteration. Gather input for validation and make adjustments accordingly.
Preliminary Sketches
Following these considerations, I have created several sketches envisioning the potential directions for the logo redesign, taking into account the modernization of the wizard icon and color scheme while maintaining the essence of Father Michael McGivney Catholic Academy's branding.
Final Design
After careful deliberation and iterative adjustments, I curated the finalization of the new logo design for Father Michael McGivney Catholic Academy. This design seamlessly blends modernized elements, including an updated wizard icon and refined color scheme, while preserving the school's distinctive identity.

Versatility is Key Timelessness Over Trends Balancing Tradition and Modernity
Despite having the creative freedom to explore new iterations, paying homage to the original logo concept emerged as a priority, informed by insightful interviews with alumni and students. The saying goes, ‚ÄúIf it‚Äôs not broken, don‚Äôt fix it.‚Äù While there were many aspects that needed improving, I wanted to maintain most aspects of the original visual identity of the logo.
The challenge with the original logo stemmed from its reflection of design trends prevalent in the early 2000s, reminiscent of the limitations imposed by older design software such as Microsoft Paint. Recognizing the evolving landscape of design practices, I grasped the significance of embracing change. Prioritizing a timeless quality in design became crucial, steering clear of overly trendy elements that could swiftly fall out of favour and ensuring the logo's longevity.
Considering the context of this logo, I found it difficult to create a design that could be used for various purposes. It was important to ensure the logo's adaptability across a diverse array of platforms to guarantee its efficacy and impact. Witnessing the evolution of my design has been a gratifying experience. It's particularly rewarding to see the logo adorning school apparel, merchandise, and murals, solidifying its presence in various facets of the school community.
